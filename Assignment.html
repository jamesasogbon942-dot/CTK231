Major in Creative Technologies

The interdisciplinary Creative Technologies major emphasizes design and practice in the integration of digital technologies and the fine arts. Its curriculum includes sequences in Game Design, Audio and Music Production, and Interaction/Information Technologies. Students will study and practice topics including digital media, sound, video, electronic music, computer graphics, animation, interactivity, creative coding, immersive media, music composition, and emerging creative technologies.

Retention Requirements

  • Students in the Audio and Music Production and Interaction/Information Technologies sequences will be allowed to continue if they maintain a cumulative GPA of 2.75 and earn a grade of C or better in all CTK courses.
  • Students in the Game Design sequence must maintain a cumulative GPA of 3.0 and a grade of C or better in all CTK courses.
  • Failure to meet retention requirements may result in removal from the program.

Standards for Progress in the Major

Students will be allowed to continue in the Creative Technologies program if they meet retention requirements and demonstrate satisfactory progress toward completion of the program.

Sequences in Creative Technologies

Accelerated Creative Technologies Sequence

High-achieving students in Creative Technologies (CTK) with a GPA of 3.5 or higher may apply to the accelerated sequence prior to the end of their junior year. This sequence allows a student to earn 12 hours of graduate credit in CTK while completing their undergraduate degree.

Audio and Music Production Sequence

The Audio and Music Production sequence provides comprehensive study in sound recording, music production, and digital audio. Courses cover recording techniques, audio theory, mixing, mastering, and creative audio production.

Game Design Sequence

The Game Design sequence emphasizes both the creative and technical aspects of video game design and development. Students study game play mechanics, interactive storytelling, level design, user experience, and immersive technologies such as virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR).

Interaction/Information Technologies Sequence

This sequence emphasizes interactive and immersive media such as mobile apps, computer programming, creative coding, virtual reality, augmented reality, and interactive installations. Students explore how digital technologies can be used in creative practice.

Creative Technologies Courses (CTK)

100-Level Classes

  • 201 – Foundations: Audio Theory (3)
    Introduces the fundamentals of audio theory as applied to the physics and aesthetics of sound.
  • 202 – Foundations: Recording and Sampling (3)
    Teaches techniques and methods used in the recording and sampling of sound.
  • 203 – Foundations: Music Concepts (3)
    Explores the study of fundamental music concepts including theory, history, genres, and form.
  • 204 – Music Production I (3)
    Introduces students to production techniques using digital audio workstations (DAWs).

200-Level Classes

  • 260 – Computer Programming for Creatives (3)
    Introduces basic programming concepts for creatives, focusing on interactive design, installation, and multimedia.
  • 261 – Digital Design I: Imaging, Digital Audio, and Video (3)
    Introduces digital media production including image editing, audio design, and video editing.
  • 262 – Live Performance (3)
    Explores interactive performance integrating computer technology with live audio, video, and stage production.

300-Level Classes

  • 301 – Advanced Web-Based Design and Development (3)
    Advanced programming concepts for interactive and dynamically generated web and multimedia applications.
  • 320 – Topics in Creative Technologies (3)
    Explores advanced issues in creative technologies through selected topics such as virtual reality, generative art, or interactive installations.
  • 340 – Advanced Game Design (3)
    Builds on foundational design skills with advanced techniques in game mechanics, level design, and immersive environments.
